I am **Kehinde (Kenny) Ademolu** — an enterprise AI engineering and platform leader with deep expertise in cyber architecture, digital resilience, and agentic AI systems.

My work sits at the intersection of three domains that rarely meet in one practice:

- **Cyber architecture** — designing secure, resilient systems for complex organisations
- **AI-native engineering** — building production systems where AI agents coordinate, decide, and act
- **Simulation and scoring** — making resilience, readiness, and decision quality measurable

## Approach

I believe the best way to understand a system is to build it, and the best way to trust a system is to break it in public and show what survived.

I use hackathons and public build logs as laboratories. [Aftershock](https://bluntmachetti.github.io/aftershock), [IncidentGym](https://bluntmachetti.github.io/incidentgym), and [Arena](https://blog.redoubtlabs.dev/arena) are not side projects — they are structured experiments that test hypotheses about multi-agent coordination, deterministic simulation, and evidence-based scoring.

The patterns developed in these laboratories feed directly into enterprise work: operational resilience modelling, incident response readiness, and AI governance at scale.

## Current focus

- **Enterprise AI platforms** — production-grade systems integrating LLMs, deterministic simulation, and multi-agent orchestration
- **Operational resilience** — making organisational readiness measurable through simulation and scoring
- **Agentic AI governance** — understanding how AI agents make decisions at scale and what guardrails hold
